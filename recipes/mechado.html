Mechado

Description

Mechado is a Filipino beef stew made with tomato sauce, potatoes, carrots, and onions. It is a popular dish in the Philippines and is often served during special occasions. It is best served with steamed rice.

Steps

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cups tomato sauce
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 large potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and cubed
  • 1 bell pepper, seeded and sliced
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine beef, soy sauce, calamansi juice, and ground black pepper. Marinate for 30 minutes.
  2. In a pot over medium heat, heat oil. Add garlic and onion and cook until softened.
  3. Add marinated beef and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned.
  4. Add tomato sauce and beef broth. Bring to a boil, skimming scum that floats on top.
  5. Lower heat, cover, and simmer for about 1 1/2 to 2 hours or until beef is fork-tender, adding more broth in 1/2 cup increments as needed.
